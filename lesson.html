Major Concepts & Terms

Before analyzing specific breeds, we must understand the language of the industry. The primary concept is "Form Follows Function"‚Äîa draft horse is built to pull, while a racing horse is built to run.

The "Hand" (Measurement) ‚ñº
Horses are measured from the ground to the withers (top of shoulder). 1 Hand = 4 Inches.
Example: A 15hh horse is 60 inches tall.
Hot vs. Cold Bloods ‚ñº
Hot-Bloods: High energy, thin skin, bred for speed (e.g., Arabian).
Cold-Bloods: Calm temperament, heavy bone, bred for work (e.g., Clydesdale).
Warmbloods: A cross of both for Olympic sport.
Gaited Horses ‚ñº
Most horses Walk, Trot, Canter. Gaited horses perform an intermediate 4-beat gait (like a fast walk) that is incredibly smooth to ride.
